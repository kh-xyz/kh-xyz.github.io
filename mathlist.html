More indepth about my studies in math in university (+ some of my personal studies).



  • My topological knowledge spans all of Väisälä I-II (metric spaces + point set), any basic course in algebraic topology (homology), Morse theory, degree + index theory, de Rham theory / cohomology, homotopy theory up to Hopf Fibrations and calculating higher homotopy groups.
  • My geometric knowledge spans across most imporant stuff covered in Lee's differential geometry, most stuff in Lee's Riemannian geometry + some topics in Petersen's Riemannian geometry (such as the Bocher Technique). I have very basic knowledge of complex manifolds.
  • My algebraic knowledge spans group theory up to Sylow theorems and basics of representation theory of finite groups. In field theory I am up to Galois theory / basic class field theory. In (commutative + identity) ring theory I know basics for algebraic geometry stuff. I know essentially all the basic results in algebra.
  • Speficily in algebraic geometry I know the basics of classical theory, and some scheme theory. This includes some stuff in Miranda's book on Algebraic curves and Riemann surfaces, and Osserman's notes on Varieties.
  • In category theory im up to Riehl's book chapter 4.
  • In probability theory I have covered Williams' probability with martingales (with only little bit of the martingales part)
  • In analysis I know basics of real and complex analysis at MSc level. More deeper knowledge I hold in functional analysis, where I have studied stuff such as Hilbert-/Banach space theory, distributions, LCTVS, spectral theory, Modern theory of PDE.
  • In mathematical logic I have covered Enderton's book on set theory which covers all of ZFC from scratch.
  • In discrete mathematics I have studied graph theory at MSc level (essentially Diestel's book).
  • I have tried to learn lean 4 formalizing at the course hosted at Aalto university, but I couldn't find the time to complete a real project. But I am aware of how all the tactics work.
